SCIENTIFIC OVERVIEW

MECHANISM OF ACTION

HGH 191AA is a recombinant polypeptide that mirrors the amino acid sequence of natural somatotropin. In cellular models, it binds to growth hormone receptors (GHR), activating the JAK2/STAT5 signaling cascade. This stimulates downstream transcriptional changes, including those regulating insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) synthesis in hepatocytes.

NEUROLOGICAL AND COGNITIVE PATHWAYS

  • Interacts with hypothalamic GHRs, influencing dopaminergic and GABAergic signaling
  • In vitro evidence suggests modulation of NMDA receptor density in hippocampal neurons
  • May alter synaptic plasticity through STAT5-mediated transcription in cortical networks
  • Influences neurotrophic factors such as BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) via IGF-1 signaling
  • Demonstrates potential regulation of circadian rhythm through hypothalamic-pituitary feedback loops

RESEARCH APPLICATIONS

HGH 191AA is studied in controlled environments for its effects on cellular regeneration, receptor-mediated signaling pathways, and neuroendocrine regulation. Investigations include models of synaptic remodeling, dopaminergic system modulation, and neuroprotective potential via IGF-1–linked cascades. For laboratory research only. Not for human consumption.

PHARMACOKINETICS

Peak systemic levels observed ~3–5 hours post-administration
Bioavailability approximated at 70–80% in animal trials
Clearance pathways: hepatic and renal degradation
