SCIENTIFIC OVERVIEW

MECHANISM OF ACTION

TB-500 is a synthetic version of a naturally occurring peptide, Thymosin Beta-4, which plays a role in tissue repair and regeneration. TB-500 interacts with actin, a key structural protein, promoting cell migration and angiogenesis, thereby accelerating wound healing and tissue recovery in preclinical studies.

NEUROLOGICAL AND COGNITIVE PATHWAYS

  • May support recovery from neural tissue injury by promoting cytoskeletal remodeling
  • Influences cellular signaling involved in cell migration and differentiation
  • Potential indirect effects on neurogenesis due to improved vascularization and tissue repair

RESEARCH APPLICATIONS

TB-500 is commonly used in research on tissue repair, wound healing, muscle recovery, and fibrosis prevention. Preclinical studies suggest enhanced cellular migration, angiogenesis, and tissue regeneration. For laboratory research only. Not for human consumption.

PHARMACOKINETICS

Half-life: Approximately 2–6 hours
Peak plasma concentrations: Within 1–2 hours post-administration
Clearance: Primarily renal with minimal hepatic metabolism
Distribution: Rapidly diffuses into tissues, particularly injured or regenerating tissue

STORAGE & HANDLING

Store lyophilized powder at 2–8°C. Once reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, maintain at 2–8°C and use within 14 days. Protect from light and avoid freezing. Handle with appropriate laboratory safety equipment.
