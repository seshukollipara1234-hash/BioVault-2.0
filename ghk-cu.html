SCIENTIFIC OVERVIEW

MECHANISM OF ACTION

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring tripeptide (glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine) complexed with copper. It binds to copper ions, forming a GHK-Cu complex which modulates gene expression in dermal fibroblasts, keratinocytes, and neuronal cells. This peptide has been shown to upregulate genes responsible for tissue remodeling, anti-inflammatory activity, and antioxidant defense.

NEUROLOGICAL AND COGNITIVE PATHWAYS

  • Interacts with NMDA receptor pathways in neural tissue
  • Modulates neuronal copper homeostasis, potentially affecting synaptic plasticity
  • Influences neurotrophic factors like BDNF and NGF
  • Exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects in neural microenvironments

RESEARCH APPLICATIONS

GHK-Cu is widely used in studies for tissue regeneration, neuroprotection, wound healing, and gene expression modulation. For laboratory research only. Not for human consumption.

PHARMACOKINETICS

Half-life: ~10–20 minutes (in vitro/animal models)
Clearance: Rapid proteolytic degradation
Bioavailability: Dependent on delivery route and tissue penetration

STORAGE & HANDLING

Store lyophilized peptide at 2–8°C. Reconstitute with sterile water as needed, keep refrigerated, and use within 7 days. Protect from light and avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles.
